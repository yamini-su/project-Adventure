NEW ZEALAND

ROUTE BURN TRACK

This short yet splendid 20 miles long trail passes through two diverse and awe-inspiring national parks, namely Mt. Aspiring and Fiordland. Trekking here offers a wonderful opportunity to explore the stunning views of magnificent peaks, lakes, rivers, waterfalls, and the superb spectacle of wild birds.


WHAT'S SO SPECIAL ABOUT IT?

As you begin to climb the highly accessible and admired Routeburn Track, the spectacular views of Hollyford Valley and Darran Mountains spellbind you. Welcome to the alpine adventure of New Zeeland!

BEST TIME TO TREK

The best hiking begins in spring (September to November in the Southern Hemisphere) when vast fields of the famous Mount Cook buttercup (Ranunculus lyallii) are in full flower. Spring temperature averages range from 9C (48F) - 17C (62F).


SWITZERLAND

HAUTE ROUTE

It seems the marvellous French architecture has been inspired from the revered ‘The Haute Route.’ This brilliant trekking trail begins at Chamonix in France and meanders through the southern Valais into Zermatt in Switzerland.


WHAT'S SO SPECIAL ABOUT IT?

This enchanted route offers picturesque views of the highest pathways available to hikers. This physically demanding European Alps hiking takes 2 weeks to finish.

BEST TIME TO TREK

Haute Route is best done in the alpine summer walking season. That's high season in July and August, with June and September still a good possibility. Snow could remain on higher passes well into July.


AMERICA

TORRESS DEL CIRCUIT

Trekking in Torres del Paine is different than anywhere else in the world. You can pretty much count on very strong winds, rain, mud, and sometimes even snow. It sounds extreme but with the proper gear and preparation it’s a lot easier to enjoy it.


WHAT'S SO SPECIAL ABOUT IT?

The 100km long trail of the Torres del Circuit offers stunning views of pink granite spires, jagged mountains, and dramatic alpine landscapes. A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, this route offers panoramic mountain views, glittering glacial lakes, magnificent Magellenic forest, and exceptional wildlife.

BEST TIME TO TREK

The best time to visit Chilean Patagonia is November to early March (summertime in the southern hemisphere). Although you'll compete with heavy crowds during this peak season, the weather is ideal for exploring Torres del Paine National Park and Tierra del Fuego.


FRANCE

GRAND RANDONEE20(GR20)

GR20 is not only counted amongst the world’s top scenic treks, but also the most exigent for both professionals and beginners. This celebrated trail is popular for its diverse landscapes, as it navigates the lofty mountains, ridges, and granite slopes.


WHAT'S SO SPECIAL ABOUT IT?

Experience the Shepherds lifestyle en route, as you meander through tricky passages and enjoy the pristine views of snow-clad peaks, glacial lakes, and vivid forests. You would simply love the challenge to traverse the rocky and steep pathways in France.

BEST TIME TO TREK

The best time for the GR20 trek is late June and early September, when most huts are open, weather is not so hot and the trail is less crowded than in July or August. Between November and May there is snow in the mountains and the trail is quite dangerous.


NEPAL

EVEREST

Everest is shaped like a three-sided pyramid. The three generally flat planes constituting the sides are called faces, and the line by which two faces join is known as a ridge. The southern route via the Khumbu Icefall and the South Col is the one most commonly taken by climbers attempting to summit Everest.


WHAT'S SO SPECIAL ABOUT IT?

This trek originates from the Khumbu region in Nepal Himalayans, where the three majestic mountains namely, Mount Everest, Lhotse Sar, and Mount Lhotse spellbind you. En route, get close and personal with oldest Buddhist Monasteries and Sherpa villages.

BEST TIME TO TREK

The best one is in April and May, right before the monsoon. Once the monsoon comes, the snow is too soft and the likelihood of avalanche too great. For a few weeks in September, after the monsoon, weather conditions may also permit an attempt; by October, however, the winter storms begin and persist until March, making climbing then nearly impossible.
